                                   Abstract

Finding the shortest route is very helpful using algorithms. Cost, time, and distance are a few
variables that can affect which algorithm is the best for determining the shortest path. This
study intends to model the optimum travel paths using Prim's algorithm, which is applica-
ble in any tourism city. In this project, we will look at the cities which offer several tourist
attractions. This approach is meant to assist tour firms in any tourism city in establishing
the optimum travel paths. With the greedy approach for choosing a subset of edges so that a
spanning tree is created and the weight of the edges is kept to a minimum, the prim's algorithm
is explored for the shortest path problem. A decision must be feasible at every stage of the
undirected graph because it is made based on what seems best at the moment and cannot
be changed at a later stage. There are several shortest-path algorithms accessible. Based on
the design of the algorithm, Prim's algorithm can be more effective and distance efficient than
other well-known methods. This algorithm can make it simpler for individuals to find tourist
attractions and boost tourism.
Keywords: - Spanning tree, edge, greedy, shortest path, undirected graph.

1.3 Aim
    · Simplify property transactions by providing a user-friendly platform [1].

    · Enhance convenience and accessibility in the real estate market.

1.4 Objective

· Enhance Visibility: Create a centralized database where rescue organizations can show-
  case their available pets, including relevant details such as breed, age, and medical history.
  This will increase the visibility of available pets and make it easier for potential adopters
  to find suitable matches[3].

· Streamline Matching Process: Develop an efficient search and filtering system that enables
  potential adopters to search for pets based on their preferences and requirements. By
  streamlining the matching process, the project aims to connect adopters with pets that
  align with their specific needs.

· Facilitate Communication: Establish a communication platform that allows for efficient
  and transparent communication between rescue organizations and potential adopters.
  This will enable better understanding, exchange of information, and the opportunity to
  address any queries or concerns, ultimately enhancing the adoption experience for both
  parties [4].

· Promote Responsible Pet Ownership: Provide potential adopters with essential informa-
  tion about the pets they are interested in, including medical history, behavioural traits,
  and specific needs. This will promote responsible pet ownership by ensuring that adopters
  have a comprehensive understanding of the pet's requirements and can make informed
  decisions.

· Increase Successful Adoptions: By improving the accessibility and transparency of the
  pet adoption process, the project aims to increase the number of successful adoptions.
  The ultimate objective is to find loving homes for as many animals as possible and reduce
  the population of homeless pets [5].

1.5 Problem Statement

  The problem addressed by the Home Selling and Home Rental Portal using PHP and MySQL
is the complexity and inefficiency in property transactions. The portal aims to streamline
property transactions by providing a user-friendly platform that connects property owners with
potential buyers and renters, offers advanced search functionalities, and ensures secure commu-
nication. The traditional process lacks a centralized platform, making it difficult for property
owners to manage listings and for buyers and renters to find suitable properties. Limited access
to property information, inefficient communication channels, and manual interactions further
hinder the process. It addresses the need for a simplified, efficient, and convenient solution in
the real estate market.

7.2 Methodologies of Problem solving

  The technique for this study was composed of three stages: data collection, data conversion,
and application of the converted information to the prim's method programming code.

7.2.1 A. Data Collection

  For efficiency and simplicity, we have collected more than 100 tourism sites and narrowed
them down to the 24 most popular sites. This strategy adheres to the fundamental principle
of increasing business profitability via effectiveness based simply on the most popular sites. A
list of locations represents an array of vertices in prim's method, where the group of distances
refers to a group of edges [7].

  The data is acquired using geolocation (latitude and longitude).

                 Figure 2: Matrix representation for the input to Prim's algorithm

7.2.2 B. Data conversion

  This section comprises two conversions: 1) translating the geolocation data to kilometres
using a normal earth radius of 6367.0 km, and 2) turning the data to an adjacency matrix.

  In Fig,which is a snapshot of the data matrix, the kilometers between two sites are shown
[8].

10 Conclusion & Future Work

10.1 Conclusion
The key difficulty, as indicated in the introduction, is determining the most effective tour
itineraries, or in this example, the shortest paths of short passes. This study uses Prim's
algorithm and uses 6 or 7 tourism locations in cities throughout INDIA as part of the initial
inputs to try to find an answer to such a challenge. These original inputs are also utilised and
turned into a two-dimensional proximity matrix as the input to the Prim's method. Eventually,
the algorithm can solve the problem by attempting to find the shortest pathways of a weeklong
journey.
